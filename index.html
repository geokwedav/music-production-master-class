Course Objectives

Step by steps goals for professional audio production.

Computer hardware and software were expensive in the seventies, eighties, and even early nineties. Only top-notch bands like Tangerine Dream, Emerson Lake and Palmer, and musicians like Herbie Hancock and Stevie Wonder could get access to such massive and expensive instruments, of course, by courtesy of the manufacturers, thus, straight from the factory.


At present, with the availability of affordable computers and software at our disposal, anyone can make music and I believe, must be encouraged to make music, for fun, or even as a profession.


Besides the availability of computer hardware and software, several methods have been written over the past few years on how to publish and sell your audio work to the public.


The aim of this course is to look at all the possibilities in electronic music production and processes such as setting up a computer-based home studio, acquiring the necessary tools and gadget, getting a deep dive into the essentials of monitoring and mixing. In addition, basic composing and arranging music and the mechanics of music, as well as ways of publishing your masterpiece will be covered. Let us have fun together for a hobby that is also enriching for the soul!
